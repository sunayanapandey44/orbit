About

Orbit Engineering Hacks is an exciting 48-hour hackathon that encourages participants to build innovative and functional solutions to real-world problems. Over the course of two days, teams will work on developing a project and delivering a presentation to showcase their solution. The event is open to high school graduates and undergraduate students in Engineering, IT, and related fields.

Structure

1. Activity: Develop a prototype or solution for a problem within 48 hours.

2. Team Size: Teams can have 1-5 participants.

3. Technology Stack: Participants are free to use any programming languages, frameworks, or tools of their choice.

4. Submission: Teams must submit both their project and a corresponding presentation within the 48-hour time limit.

5. Judging Criteria:

Innovation(30%): Originality and creativity of the solution.

Functionality(30%): How well the solution works and its real-world applicability.

Technical Complexity (20%): Depth and complexity of the technology used.

Presentation(20%): How well the team presents their solution and the clarity of their explanation.

FAQs

1. Who can participate?
High school graduates and undergraduate students in Engineering, IT, and related fields.

2. Can I participate alone?
Yes, participants can join solo or in teams of up to five members.

3. What technologies can be used?
Any programming languages, frameworks, and tools can be used.

4. Will internet access be provided?
Yes, internet access will be available throughout the event.

5. How will the projects be judged?
Projects will be judged based on innovation, functionality, technical complexity, and the quality of the final presentation.

Rules

1. Team Size: Teams can have 1-5 participants.

2. Duration: The hackathon lasts 48 hours.

3. Submission: Projects and presentations must be submitted before the 48-hour deadline.

4. Tools: Teams are free to use any programming languages, frameworks, or development tools.

5. Originality: All solutions must be original and developed during the hackathon; any form of plagiarism will result in disqualification.

6. Decision: The judges' decisions are final.
